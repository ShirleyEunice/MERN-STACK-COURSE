PAPHIOPEDILUM

OVERVIEW

Paphiopedilum, commonly known as the Slipper Orchid, is a unique and fascinating type of orchid known for its slipper-shaped pouch, which serves as a trap for pollinators. Native to parts of Southeast Asia, India, and China, these terrestrial orchids grow in the forest understory, often in leaf litter or mossy areas. The pouch-shaped lip of the flower is designed to trap insects, forcing them to climb through the flower, facilitating pollination.

Key characteristics include:

  • Flowers: Distinctive slipper-shaped lip (labellum) with vibrant colors and intricate patterns. Some varieties also feature mottled or striped leaves.
  • Blooming Cycle: Typically blooms once a year, with flowers lasting several weeks.
  • Care: They prefer low to medium light, cooler temperatures, and moderate humidity. They require regular watering, but the soil should be well-draining to avoid root rot.
  • Symbolism: Paphiopedilum orchids symbolize rarity and luxury due to their exotic appearance and somewhat finicky care requirements.
