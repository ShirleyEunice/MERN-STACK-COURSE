PHALAENOPSIS

OVERVIEW

Phalaenopsis, commonly known as the Moth Orchid, is one of the most popular and widely cultivated types of orchids due to its ease of care and long-lasting blooms. Native to tropical Asia, these orchids are typically epiphytic, meaning they grow on trees in their natural environment. Phalaenopsis orchids are prized for their broad, flat leaves and striking, butterfly-like flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, yellow, and purple.

Key characteristics include:

  • Flowers: Large, round, and can last for several months.
  • Blooming Cycle: They often bloom once or twice a year, with flowers lasting up to 3-6 months under the right conditions.
  • Care: They prefer bright, indirect light, high humidity, and moderate watering. They're known to thrive indoors, making them ideal for home gardeners.
  • Symbolism: Phalaenopsis orchids often symbolize beauty, elegance, and refinement.
